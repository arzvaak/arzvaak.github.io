1.4 Division with Quotient and Remainder

üèõÔ∏è Division Algorithm

For integers a and b (where b ‚â† 0):

$$a = b \times q + r$$

where:

  • q = quotient (integer number of times b goes into a)
  • r = remainder (what's left over)
  • 0 ‚â§ r < b

üí° Example

Divide 17 by 5:

17 = 5 √ó 3 + 2
  • Quotient (q) = 3
  • Remainder (r) = 2

Modulo Operation

üìö Definition

a mod b = remainder when a is divided by b

Example 1:

17 mod 5 = 2

Since 17 = 5 √ó 3 + 2
Example 2:

20 mod 6 = 2

Since 20 = 6 √ó 3 + 2
Example 3:

15 mod 3 = 0

Since 15 = 3 √ó 5 + 0

1.5 Divisibility

üìö Definition

We say a | b (a divides b) if a mod b = 0

This means b is divisible by a with no remainder.

Factors

Factors of a number n are all numbers that divide n evenly (with remainder 0).

üí° Example: Factors of 24

Let's find all numbers that divide 24:

2 | 24 ‚úì (24 √∑ 2 = 12)

Factors of 24: {1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 8, 12, 24}

Problem 1: Division Algorithm

Find the quotient and remainder when 47 is divided by 6.

Solution:

47 = 6 √ó 7 + 5

  • Quotient = 7
  • Remainder = 5
  • Verification: 6 √ó 7 + 5 = 42 + 5 = 47 ‚úì

Problem 2: Modulo Operation

Calculate: (a) 23 mod 7, (b) 100 mod 9, (c) 50 mod 10

Solutions:

  • (a) 23 mod 7 = 2 (since 23 = 7 √ó 3 + 2)
  • (b) 100 mod 9 = 1 (since 100 = 9 √ó 11 + 1)
  • (c) 50 mod 10 = 0 (since 50 = 10 √ó 5 + 0)
