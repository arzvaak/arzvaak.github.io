1. Calculus Fundamentals

1.1 Limits and Continuity

Definition of Limit

The limit of a function f(x) as x approaches a value 'a' is:

$$\lim_{x \to a} f(x) = L$$

This means f(x) gets arbitrarily close to L as x gets arbitrarily close to a.

Properties of Limits

  • Sum Rule: $$\lim_{x \to a} [f(x) + g(x)] = \lim_{x \to a} f(x) + \lim_{x \to a} g(x)$$
  • Product Rule: $$\lim_{x \to a} [f(x) \cdot g(x)] = \lim_{x \to a} f(x) \cdot \lim_{x \to a} g(x)$$
  • Quotient Rule: $$\lim_{x \to a} \frac{f(x)}{g(x)} = \frac{\lim_{x \to a} f(x)}{\lim_{x \to a} g(x)}$$ (if denominator ‚â† 0)

Example Problem

Find $$\lim_{x \to 2} \frac{x^2 - 4}{x - 2}$$

Solution:
1

Factor the numerator:

$$x^2 - 4 = (x+2)(x-2)$$
2

Substitute and simplify:

$$\lim_{x \to 2} \frac{(x+2)(x-2)}{x-2} = \lim_{x \to 2} (x+2)$$
3

Evaluate the limit:

$$\lim_{x \to 2} (x+2) = 2+2 = 4$$

1.2 Derivatives

Definition

The derivative of f(x) at point x is:

$$f'(x) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(x+h) - f(x)}{h}$$

Basic Derivative Rules

Constant Rule
$$\frac{d}{dx}(c) = 0$$
Power Rule
$$\frac{d}{dx}(x^n) = nx^{n-1}$$
Exponential
$$\frac{d}{dx}(e^x) = e^x$$
Logarithm
$$\frac{d}{dx}(\ln x) = \frac{1}{x}$$

2. Linear Algebra Basics

2.1 Vectors

Vector Definition

A vector is a mathematical object with both magnitude and direction.

In 2D: $$\vec{v} = \begin{pmatrix} x \\ y \end{pmatrix}$$

In 3D: $$\vec{v} = \begin{pmatrix} x \\ y \\ z \end{pmatrix}$$

Vector Operations

Addition
$$\vec{a} + \vec{b} = \begin{pmatrix} a_1 + b_1 \\ a_2 + b_2 \end{pmatrix}$$
Scalar Multiplication
$$k\vec{a} = \begin{pmatrix} ka_1 \\ ka_2 \end{pmatrix}$$
Dot Product
$$\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b} = a_1b_1 + a_2b_2$$
Magnitude
$$|\vec{a}| = \sqrt{a_1^2 + a_2^2}$$
