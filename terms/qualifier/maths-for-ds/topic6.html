7.9 Bijections and Cardinality

ü•é The Marble Analogy

Problem: Two sacks of marbles - are they equal in number?

Method 1: Count separately

Count each sack individually (tedious, error-prone)

Method 2: Pair simultaneously

Pull one marble from each sack at the same time:

  • If both empty together ‚Üí equal number (bijection established!)
  • If one empties first ‚Üí unequal

üåü For Infinite Sets

Bijections are the ONLY way to prove equal cardinality!

This is how we compare the "sizes" of infinite sets like ‚Ñï, ‚Ñ§, ‚Ñö, and ‚Ñù.

  • Bijection: lines ‚Üî ‚Ñù √ó ‚Ñù (points in plane)
  • Therefore: Same number of lines as points!

‚ö†Ô∏è Careful!

Must verify it's truly a bijection. Example: two points determine a unique line, BUT many point-pairs determine the SAME line, so point-pairs ‚Üí lines is NOT a bijection.
